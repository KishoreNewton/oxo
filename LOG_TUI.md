1. Log aggregation and observability TUI
Pain point: Datadog bills routinely reach $50K–$3.6M/year for organizations, with unpredictable pricing driven by high-water-mark billing, per-metric charges ($0.05 each, up to 52% of total bills), and dual-cost log management (pay to ingest, pay again to index). SigNoz Coinbase's $65M Datadog bill went viral on Hacker News. SigNoz +2 Developers describe it as "feature overload — powerful but cluttered." CubeAPM
What it replaces: Datadog ($15–40/host/month), CloudeagleSigNoz Papertrail, Logtail, New Relic ($349/user/year + $0.40/GB). A 50-host deployment paying Datadog $50K+/year could use self-hosted Grafana/Loki/Prometheus at 80–95% savings — but no TUI exists to make this stack accessible.
Why TUI is the right form factor: Log streams are inherently text. Real-time tailing, filtering, searching, and pattern matching are faster via keyboard than mouse. Sparkline charts, gauges, and tables render excellently in modern terminals. Ratatui The tool works over SSH, letting SREs debug production from anywhere without a browser.
Existing competition: lnav (log file navigator, not aggregator), toolong (basic log tailer), lazyjournal (very early, journalctl only). No mature TUI connects to Loki, Elasticsearch, CloudWatch, or OpenTelemetry backends. This is the single largest gap in the entire TUI ecosystem.
Estimated difficulty: High. Requires async data streaming from multiple backends (Loki/Prometheus/Elasticsearch/CloudWatch), query language parsing (LogQL, PromQL), real-time chart rendering, and alert configuration. Ratatui's async support and the tokio ecosystem make this feasible but ambitious. Estimate 4–6 months for an MVP with one backend.
Star potential: Very high (20k+ within 2 years). Combines the most expensive developer pain point with the strongest anti-SaaS sentiment. A "k9s for observability" positioning would resonate immediately.
